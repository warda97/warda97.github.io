Warda Usman, Ph.D.

I'm a mixed-methods researcher in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Online Trust & Safety. Currently, I work as a senior researcher on Walmart Inc.'s Security Operations team, where I work at the intersection of cybersecurity, user behavior, and organizational resilience. Before this, I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Brigham Young University under the supervision of Dr. Daniel Zappala. Our work was published at top-tier venues like IEEE S&P and USENIX SOUPS.

My current work focuses on understanding how people navigate security and privacy decisions in real-world contexts, particularly in enterprise environments. My research combines both qualitative and quantitative methods, and I use a wide range of methods, from in-depth interviews and ethnographic studies to large-scale surveys and behavioral analytics. I translate complex security challenges into actionable insights that balance user needs with organizational security goals.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about mentorship and building inclusive communities. I've mentored students navigating the PhD application process, particularly those from underrepresented backgrounds, and actively contribute to DEI initiatives in the tech and academic communities.
